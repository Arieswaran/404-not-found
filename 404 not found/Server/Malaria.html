Malaria

You may have Malaria


Overview

Malaria is a disease caused by a parasite, transmitted by the bite of infected mosquitoes. Malaria produces recurrent attacks of chills and fever. Malaria kills an estimated 660,000 people each year

Symptoms

  • Moderate to severe shaking chills
  • High fever
  • Sweating
  • Headache
  • vomiting
  • Diarrhea

Causes

Malaria is caused by a type of microscopic parasite that's transmitted most commonly by mosquito bites.

Cure

The only way is you have to see doctor.

Prevention

  • If you're going to be traveling to a location where malaria is common, talk to your doctor a few months ahead of time about drugs you can take — before, during and after your trip — that can help protect you from malaria parasites.
  • In general, the drugs taken to prevent malaria are the same drugs used to treat the disease
